Artificial intelligence (AI) systems are increasingly used in recruitment, particularly for CV screening. However, despite their appearance of neutrality, these technologies can replicate and even amplify human biases. One of the main concerns about using AI in CV sorting is how it may perpetuate discrimination based on characteristics such as age, gender, or ethnicity.

For example, an AI trained on historical data could learn to favor white candidates over 50 if, in the past, this group was overrepresented in positions being filled. The algorithm, basing its conclusions on these data, might determine that this profile has the highest chance of success, even though it merely reflects existing biases and discriminatory practices within the recruitment process. This bias can manifest subtly, such as by overvaluing certain experiences or qualifications that statistically tend to be more common among people from this group.

Similarly, for positions perceived as traditionally female, such as nursing roles, an AI might favor CVs from women. If historical data show that the majority of people holding these positions were women, the algorithm might develop an association between female gender and success in such roles, potentially excluding qualified male candidates.

These biases are not intentional but are inherent to the functioning of AIs that rely on past data. If the training data reflect inequalities or stereotypes, the algorithm’s decisions will be biased. Therefore, it is crucial to use diverse data and implement bias correction mechanisms to prevent AI from reproducing discriminatory patterns. Companies must also remain vigilant by regularly evaluating the algorithms used in their recruitment processes to ensure these tools contribute to greater fairness, rather than perpetuating existing inequalities.

In summary, using AI in CV selection poses risks of biases related to age, gender, and ethnicity. It is essential to take measures to ensure that these tools do not become amplifiers of injustices but rather leverage inclusion and diversity.
